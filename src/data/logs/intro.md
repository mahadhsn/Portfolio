# Intro: why I started writing

_June 21, 2025 · 4 min read · life_

![Norway's Fjords, August 2018](/images/logs/intro/norway18.jpg)

Hello again :) Not sure how many people will even read this but I appreciate you for doing so! On the logbook section, I will be documenting my life more in depth (I hope). There are a few rules I'll be following:

- **NO AI!**: We often blindly use AI in everything nowadays and I feel it makes it difficult for us to be authentic. You'll be seeing me, myself, and I on this blob of the internet.
- **NO FILTER!**: I'm going to attempt to be as transparent as possible about my views, opinions, and experiences. Luckily no politics will be discussed.

So that's all I can think of for now but this'll likely be the only part of the logbook that might be edited in the future. I'll also try to keep at least one image per log to retain attention for today's generation.

## Why?

Frankly, idk. If you think about it, this page is in the depths of the internet on a page not a lot of people would visit. That gives it a nice bit of balance from being both too public or too private. Maybe it's a sort of public diary which gives me a weird sense of comfort?

I'm also at a point in life where I want to try out new things, cause why not, you know? What's the worst that could happen? Maybe my future employer will see this and think "lmao what is this guy doing?" and throw my resume in the trash. BUT, they could also think "wow, he took a risk, dude seems interesting." There's a million things that COULD happen. Why not focus on what's in your hand?

At the end of the day, I keep itching towards the idea of not caring about the opinions of others, not in a self-centered or rude way, but in more of a "it's my life and I think I should do what I want" way.

Moreso, I might affect someone in a positive way. To me, there are almost no negatives to this. Either I help someone out, I learn how to finally write better, or no one ever reads this and I just have a nice place to vent my thoughts.

## Plans

At this point, I wanna write about a lot but you know how that usually goes. My current plans are to write about 3 main categories:

- **life**: personal life choices, hobbies, interests, personal growth, etc.
- **school**: academic life, course reviews, notes, exams.
- **career**: professional development, internships, the whole nine yards.

I thought of also adding any notes I made for courses which might help out someone out there. I know how much I struggled at times with those so if it can help someone, I'll be glad.

## Reflection

I'm excited to see how this goes. I've seen countless portfolios on the internet with logbooks/blogs and they're always interesting to skim through. It almost feels like a 1P13 reflection but thank god this one isn't graded and I can ramble on about whatever I feel like.

If you made it this far, I appreciate you. If you have any feedback, suggestions, or just wanna chat, feel free to reach out through the [contact page](/contact).
