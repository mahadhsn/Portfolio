ScleroCare is a mobile app built in partnership with Scleroderma Canada and the McMaster iBioMed Society through the Tech4Good program. Scleroderma is a rare autoimmune disease, and patients often struggle to find centralized, reliable resources for their care.

The app brings together clinical trial information, workshops, support libraries for patients and caregivers, a location-aware map of nearby points of interest, and a fully encrypted Medical Resume — all in one place.

## Building for real users

This was my first time building a production mobile app. I had never touched React Native before taking this on, and I was simultaneously studying full-time and involved in three other clubs. The learning curve was steep but the stakes were real: actual patients would use this.

The encrypted Medical Resume was the most technically interesting part. We implemented end-to-end encryption for sensitive health data, making sure it was protected both in transit and at rest in Firebase.

## What's next

The app is currently in final testing and is on track for release on the App Store. Working on something that will genuinely help people navigate a difficult diagnosis has been one of the most meaningful projects I've been a part of.
